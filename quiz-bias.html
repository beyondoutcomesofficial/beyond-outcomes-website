Bhagavad Gita · Chapter 3 · The Veils of the Mind

What Is Your Blind Spot?

The Gita teaches that the mind does not see reality — it sees a version of reality shaped by its own patterns, desires, and fears. In NLP this is called filtering. In psychology, cognitive bias. Whatever the name, the pattern is the same: your mind has a favourite way of distorting the truth. This inquiry helps you find yours.

The Three Blind Spots
  • Pattern Seeker — You see what you expect to see.
    A powerful ability to find meaning — that can become a prison when the pattern isn't really there.
  • Story Teller — You make meaning through narrative.
    Rich inner world, deep sense-making — sometimes at the cost of seeing things as they actually are.
  • Loss Avoider — You feel losses more sharply than gains.
    A survival instinct that can keep you holding on long past the point of wisdom.
"The map is not the territory. Your blind spot is where your map and reality diverge most — and where the most growth waits."
— R. Satyamoorthy · Certified NLP Practitioner
