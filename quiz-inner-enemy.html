Bhagavad Gita · Chapters 3 & 16 · The Four Inner Obstacles

What Veils Your Wisdom?

The Gita identifies four primary veils over human wisdom — Kama, Krodha, Lobha, and Moha. These are not moral failures but energetic patterns that cloud the clarity already within you. This inquiry reveals which obstacle most veils your inner knowing right now.

The Four Veils
  • Kama — Craving — The fire of wanting that is never fully quenched. Desire that binds the mind to what it doesn't have.
  • Krodha — Anger — The heat that blinds and drives reckless action. Frustration turned inward and outward.
  • Lobha — Clinging — The grip that holds too tight to what it has. Fear of loss dressed as love.
  • Moha — Delusion — The fog that obscures what is real from what is imagined. The deepest veil of all.
