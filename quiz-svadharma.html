Bhagavad Gita · Chapter 18 · The Four Natures

What Is Your Svadharma Nature?

The Gita teaches that each person is born with a natural orientation — a svabhava, an innate quality — that shapes their dharma. This is not birth-caste but nature-type: the way you are wired to engage with the world. BG 18.41–44 describes four essential types. This inquiry reveals yours.

The Four Svadharma Natures
  • Seeker · Teacher — Drawn to knowledge, inquiry, and passing wisdom forward. The inner light of understanding.
  • Warrior · Leader — Drawn to courage, protection, and decisive leadership. The strength that shields others.
  • Builder · Creator — Drawn to creation, commerce, and abundance. The vision that builds something lasting.
  • Server · Nurturer — Drawn to service, care, and devotion. The quiet force that holds everything together.
